## 1. Definitions

For the purposes of this policy, and in accordance with the GDPR (art. 4) and the Swiss FADP (art. 5):

- **AFianco** (or "the Platform"): the web-based Business Intelligence service accessible at https://afianco.app.
- **User** (or "Data Subject"): the natural person whose personal data is being processed. Includes: (a) the **Administrator** of the organization that registers with AFianco; (b) the **Team Members** invited by the Administrator; (c) the **end-customers** of the Commerce Module (see art. 18); (d) the **business contacts** (clients, suppliers) entered by the Administrator into their datasets.
- **Organization**: the legal entity, sole proprietorship, or professional on behalf of which the User registers an account on AFianco.
- **Personal data**: any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person (art. 4(1) GDPR).
- **Processing**: any operation performed on personal data, whether automated or not (art. 4(2) GDPR).
- **Data Controller**: who determines the purposes and means of the processing (art. 4(7) GDPR).
- **Data Processor**: who processes personal data on behalf of the Controller (art. 4(8) GDPR).
- **Sub-processor**: the processor engaged by the principal Processor (art. 28(2) GDPR).
- **AI / Artificial Intelligence**: the features for analysis, chat and automatic reporting based on third-party language models integrated into the Platform.

## 7. Details on data transmitted to the AI (Anthropic)

AI features are optional. The User may use the Platform without any AI interaction.

When the User enables and uses an AI feature, AFianco transmits to Anthropic data strictly functional to the response. Transmission modes vary by feature type:

### 7.1 Digest and automatic analyses
Only **aggregated indicators** are transmitted:
- Totals per period (revenue, expenses, purchases, fixed costs)
- Operating margins and financial health score (numerical KPIs)
- Percentage trends and YoY variations
- Active alert counts

**NOT transmitted**: supplier or customer names, single transaction details, personal contacts, individual amounts per counterparty.

### 7.2 Interactive chat
When the User asks a question, the AI model accesses, through automated tools (tool-use), summaries that may include:
- Names of the top suppliers (top 5 by spending volume in the period)
- Names of the top customers (top 10 by revenue in the period)
- Aggregated expense categories
- Computed KPIs

**NOT transmitted**: phone numbers, third-party addresses, third-party emails, payment data, invoice or document content, audit logs.

### 7.3 Anthropic's processing terms
According to Anthropic's API terms of service, data transmitted via API:
- Is used exclusively to generate the requested response
- Is not used to train AI models
- Is subject to temporary retention for security and moderation purposes (maximum 30 days under the current Anthropic policy)
- Is covered by SCC and/or EU-U.S. DPF

For Anthropic's terms: https://www.anthropic.com/legal/commercial-terms

### 7.4 Automated decision-making (art. 22 GDPR)
AFianco's AI processing is **purely informational and advisory in nature**. It does not produce legal effects on the User nor does it significantly affect their person within the meaning of art. 22 GDPR. The User retains full decision-making autonomy at all times; AI analyses are support tools, not approval, rejection, scoring, or profiling automations.

## 10. Data security (art. 32 GDPR)

AFianco implements technical and organizational measures appropriate to the risk:

### 10.1 Technical measures

- **Encryption in transit**: TLS 1.2/1.3 mandatory on all connections (HTTPS), Let's Encrypt certificates; HTTP Strict Transport Security (HSTS) active
- **Password encryption**: bcrypt with 12 rounds and automatic salt; no password is ever stored in cleartext
- **Encryption at rest**: the database and backups are encrypted at the Hetzner volume level
- **Authentication tokens**: signed JWTs, with configurable expiration and automatic invalidation on password change
- **Rate limiting**: per-IP limits on authentication endpoints (5 attempts / 15 minutes)
- **Account lockout**: temporary lock on repeated failed attempts (exponential backoff)
- **Security headers**: X-Content-Type-Options, X-Frame-Options, Content-Security-Policy, Referrer-Policy
- **Webhook validation**: HMAC signature on incoming webhooks (Stripe, Brevo)
- **Multi-tenant isolation**: strict per-`organization_id` data segregation enforced on every database query; verified automatically at the ORM level
- **Email masking in logs**: partial masking of emails in logging output
- **Immutable audit log**: append-only writes on a dedicated collection
- **Automatic backups**: daily encrypted backups with 30-day rolling retention
- **Monitoring**: detection of anomalies in access patterns and brute-force attempts

### 10.2 Organizational measures

- **Principle of least privilege**: system administrators access data only for technical maintenance purposes, without authorization to view the content of User datasets
- **Role separation**: platform admins can manage accounts and subscriptions, but CANNOT view Organizations' financial data
- **Periodic audit**: periodic review of accesses, sub-processors, and security measures
- **Data breach management procedure**: defined under GDPR arts. 33-34 (see art. 14)

### 10.3 Vulnerability disclosure

If a security vulnerability is discovered in the Platform, report it to `davide@afianco.ch` with subject "Security disclosure". AFianco commits to respond within 5 business days.

### 11.8 Specific rights under the Swiss FADP

### 11.9 Right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority
The Data Subject has the right to lodge a complaint with:
- **For Switzerland residents**: Federal Data Protection and Information Commissioner (FDPIC) — https://www.edoeb.admin.ch

## 12. Methods of exercising rights

### 12.1 Self-service account deactivation

The Administrator may deactivate the account at any time from the Platform Settings. Deactivation entails:

1. **Immediately**:
   - Access block for the Administrator and all Team Members
   - Cancellation of any active Stripe subscriptions
   - Email notification to Organization members
2. **30-day grace period**: the account may be reactivated by contacting support. During this period the data is soft-deleted (not accessible but still present in the database, except for subscriptions which remain cancelled).
3. **23 days after deactivation (7 days before definitive deletion)**: sending a reminder email to the Administrator with instructions to export data (art. 11.5) or reactivate the account.
4. **30 days after deactivation**: definitive and irreversible deletion of all personal and business data of the Organization, performed automatically. Audit logs are anonymized (removal of association with personal identifiers) but kept for the residual retention period for legal protection and security purposes.

### 12.2 Requests via email

All other requests relating to one's rights should be addressed to `davide@afianco.ch`. The Controller responds within **30 days** of receipt; in case of particularly complex requests, the term may be extended by a further 60 days with motivated prior notice to the Data Subject (art. 12(3) GDPR).

To ensure the security of the request, the Controller may ask for confirmation of the Data Subject's identity (e.g. verification through the email associated with the account).

## 14. Data breach notification

In the event of a personal data breach under art. 33 GDPR (Personal Data Breach), the Controller:

1. **Within 72 hours** of becoming aware of the breach, notifies the competent supervisory authority (Switzerland: FDPIC; EU: Data Protection Authority of the country of establishment or the country of the Data Subject), unless the breach is unlikely to result in a risk to the rights and freedoms of natural persons.
2. **Without undue delay**, communicates the breach directly to the Data Subjects when it is likely to result in a high risk to their rights and freedoms (art. 34 GDPR).
3. Internally documents every breach, its consequences and the measures taken to remediate, regardless of the notification obligation.

The communication to the Data Subject includes at least: the nature of the breach, contact details of the privacy contact, likely consequences, measures taken or proposed.

## 19. Data protection by design and by default (art. 25 GDPR)

AFianco adopts the following data protection principles from the design stage:

- **Minimization**: collection of only the data strictly necessary for the stated purposes
- **Purpose limitation**: each datum is processed only for purposes compatible with those stated at collection
- **Storage limitation**: automatic TTL and explicit retention for each category
- **Privacy-friendly defaults**: AI disabled by default on new accounts; explicit consent required for activation
- **Pseudonymization**: where technically feasible, personal data is replaced with opaque identifiers (e.g. UUIDs) in logs
- **Accountability**: the immutable consent audit and the operational audit log allow demonstrating compliance of processing
